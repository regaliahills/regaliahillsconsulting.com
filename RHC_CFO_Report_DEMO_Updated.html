CFO Commentary
Prepared by Regalia Hills · Andrew P.

The quarter in three sentences.

Hearthstone is having its best year on record. Revenue is pacing toward ~$5.1M for FY26, and the deliberate push into the boutique channel is working — boutique grew +91% YoY and now carries a 44% gross margin, lifting the blended margin to 33.9%.

The one thing the leadership team should not misread is cash. The $385K balance is flattering: $312K of it is customer deposits for kitchens and additions not yet built. Spend against the $73K of genuinely free cash, not the headline. The other watch item is the commercial channel, where actual margin (30.2%) keeps landing below bid — worth a pricing review before signing the summer pipeline.

Three Things Demanding Attention

✓ Tailwind

Boutique revenue is up 91% YoY at a 44% gross margin. Every incremental boutique dollar is worth ~1.4x a residential dollar in gross profit terms — keep feeding it.

Margin Recovery · Recommended Actions
Action 01
Reprice the commercial book.
Commercial is bidding at 33% and realizing 30.2%. Either raise bids ~3 points or tighten scope definitions. Closing the gap is worth ~$20K YTD, ~$48K annualized.
Action 02
Protect the boutique premium.
Boutique's 44% margin is the franchise. Resist the urge to discount to win volume — its win rate (58%) says pricing power is real. Defend it.
Action 03
Lock material pricing earlier.
Materials ticked up 60 bps. For jobs over 8 weeks out, securing supplier quotes at contract signing rather than at start would stabilize the largest cost line.

What This Means
Read 01
The overage is an investment, not a leak.
$14K of the $22K budget overage is marketing — and it correlates directly with the +91% boutique growth. This is the right kind of variance.
Read 02
Overhead is 72% fixed — watch the floor.
Because most overhead is fixed, a revenue dip wouldn't shrink it. The current absorption depends on volume holding. Keep an eye on backlog as the leading indicator.
Read 03
Operating leverage is real and improving.
Overhead per revenue dollar fell from 27.4% in January to 21.7% in May. If revenue holds its ramp, full-year overhead should settle near 22.5%.

Cash Action Plan · Next 90 Days
Priority 01
Manage to free cash, not bank balance.
Adopt a simple weekly dashboard line: cash on hand minus unearned deposits. Capital decisions (hires, equipment) should reference the $73K figure, not the $385K.
Priority 02
Extend payables to terms.
DPO of 29 days is faster than necessary. Moving to net-35 with the top three suppliers frees ~$34K with no relationship cost — pure working-capital gain.
Priority 03
Tighten the commercial collections.
All $38K of over-60 AR is commercial. A milestone-based billing schedule with shorter terms on commercial contracts would close the only soft spot in an otherwise clean book.
